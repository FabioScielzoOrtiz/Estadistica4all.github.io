Chapter 1 Data-frame as data-matrix

We are going to consider data-frames as data matrix.

Given a data-frame \(\hspace{0.05cm}df\hspace{0.05cm}\) with \(\hspace{0.05cm}p\hspace{0.05cm}\) columns and \(\hspace{0.05cm}n\hspace{0.05cm}\) rows, we can represent it as data-matrix as follows:


\[D(df) \hspace{0.05cm} = \hspace{0.05cm} \begin{pmatrix} x_1^t \\ x_2^t \\ ... \\ x_n^t \end{pmatrix} \hspace{0.05cm} =\hspace{0.05cm} \begin{pmatrix} x_{11} & x_{12} & ... & x_{1p}\\ x_{21} & x_{22} & ... & x_{2p}\\ ... & ... & ... & ... \\ x_{n1} & x_{n2} & ... & x_{np} \end{pmatrix} \hspace{0.05cm} =\hspace{0.05cm} \left( X_1 X_2 ... X_p \right)\]


Where \(\hspace{0.05cm}x_i=(x_{i1},..., x_{ip})^t\hspace{0.05cm}\) represent the \(\hspace{0.05cm}i\)-th row and \(\hspace{0.05cm}X_j\hspace{0.05cm}\) the \(\hspace{0.05cm}j\)-th column of the data-frame \(\hspace{0.05cm}df\).
